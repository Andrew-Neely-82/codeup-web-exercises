National Park Facts

Grand Canyon is
Known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.

California's Sequoia National Park is
home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world named General Sherman.

The Great Smoky Mountains National Park is
the most visited National Park with more than 9 million guests per year.

Mount McKinley in the Alaska Range of Denali National Park is
the highest point in the national parks ar 20,302 feet.

Mammoth Cave National Park in Kentucky
has the longest cave system in the world with more than 3,454 mapped miles.

Only one state in the US is
not lucky enough to have a National park or monument. It is the first state. Delaware

In 2014, authorities at the Sequoia National Park found
a $36 million marijuana cultivation operation just half a mile from a popular tourist area

White Sands National Monument spans more than
176,000 acres of New Mexico desert and contains the world's largest gypsum dune fields

Mauna Loa Volcano in Hawaii Volcanoes National Park is the
largest volcano on earth both in terms of height and volume.

Bears have not killed a single person in Olympic National Park, but
a mountain goat, which is not a native species to the region, killed a man in 2010.
